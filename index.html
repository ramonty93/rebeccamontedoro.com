Rebecca Montedoro

Rebecca Montedoro paints toward a future she hopes will exist. Her work moves in flat planes of flowing color. Forms pool into one another; edges dissolve; the distinction between figure and ground gives way. Nature flourishes. People connect. Time softens. She treats the canvas as a place where a changed world can be seen before it arrives. She calls this prefigurative painting — a phrase borrowed from prefigurative politics, where the future is enacted in the present rather than postponed.
